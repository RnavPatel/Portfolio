UAE Expo Pavillion 2025

Envisioning the UAE’s Carbon Net-Zero goals and future-forward message through an immersive experience.

Role: Strategy, Experience, Exhibit, and Interaction Designer

Duration: 6 weeks

Tools: Interaction Design, IoT, Unity C#, Projection Mapping, Rhino, Digital Fabrication

Progress Through Unity.

Taking an empathetic approach to the Osaka Expo 2025 theme, UAE highlights its core values of unity that fosters the country’s rapid development. The concluding experience tells the story of UAE’s progress through its origin of Pearl Diving – the early Emirati society.

Representing innovation, collaboration, and progress, pearl diving is used as a metaphor to highlight the collective Emirati effort to achieve Carbon Net-Zero in 2031.

Immersion into the UAE's Story.

With many countries moving towards net-zero, the large question was how to make this message unique through the Emirati lens. This is achieved by using the motif of Society 1.0 was crucial is highlighting the content more meaningfully to the UAE’s story. The exhibit is designed to mimic the pearl diving journey, as viewers enter along the coastlineto learn the history of the UAE, receive pearl diving nets to board their ships, and set off to sea for the "Great Dive."

Understanding Emirati Culture.

The research process involved understanding multiple facets of traditional Emirati culture in balance with their future-forward initiatives. From this, I landed on UAE’s net zero goals in 2031, which represents a paradigm shift in how sustainability and longevity will be incorporated with Arab culture.

Crafting a Narrative.

The first interaction is a long “sand-table” which displays the timeline of the UAE. Inspired by the line “the coastline is constantly growing,” as an indication of the country’s rapid growth, the table allows viewers to physically build up the coastline through icon based sand-molds.

The second interaction shows the early history of pearl-diving, introducing the pearl nets viewers will take into the diving zone. A digital bowl lights up everytime a pearl is collected in the diving zone.

The diving zone is the final storytelling experience which helps viewers envision the future of the UAE through listening to wishes from students there. Students were asked what they imagine the UAE will look like, which are stored in digital pearls along the projected ocean floor. Once collected by viewers, they can listen to these wishes at one of the pearl bowls.

Pearl-Diving Inspired Interaction

Based on the concept of Pearl Diving, I wanted to engage viewers in a co-creative experience that encourages participation with content, as well as others in the room. This exploration lead me to imagine how the pearl metaphor can be translated into space, ultimately landing on creating a digital “pearl-diving” simulation for learning about the UAE through anecdotes.

Lo-Fi Prototyping Single Person

This lo-fi playtest intended to find the appropriate scale for the pearls. Some things learned during testing were that people were inclined to grab more than 1 pearl at a time with the smaller pearls. Additionally, having the colors of the pearls visible once caught made them search more.

Lo-Fi Prototyping Multi Person

When testing with two people, the experience suddenly became more competitive, and there was a need for urgency and rush because you want to "beat the other person" to the pearl. This taught me that I need a waiting interaction, and some way of making the experience more collaborative than competitive.

Refined Storyboard

From the lo-fi prototyping, this led to a refined storyboard, identifying some of the multiple cases I would need to design for in the experience: single use, multi-use, waiting, active (listening), and passive (finding).

Hi-tech Playtesting

After putting this on Unity and Motive, I found a lot editting needed to happen with scales, distances, etc. to make the experience less frustrating. Something significant was that users would innately turn their device to "drop pearls" into the basket, rather than how I orginally planned of lowering the device. This made me add a turn recognition feature that drops a pearl when you turn your device more than 100°.

Concept Development for Device

For the device, I wanted to capture qualities of traditional pearl diving nets that would be appropriate for the interaction zone. I landed on the rim of the pearl net, focusing on a sleek cool hoop. However, I wanted to have the light area shine in a way that it would emulate the pattern of the net braids underwater.

Iterating Scale

For the first iteration, I focused on the scale of the rim itself (foam), creating variations that either maximized grip control, or negative space (so see the projection under). I landed on minimizing grip to create less shadows in the interaction zone and fit multiple hand-types. For the first 3D print, the net-braid pattern was abstracted in these large arrowhead forms.

Refining Light Seams

From the first iteration, I wanted to focus more on the light pattern in capturing the quality of the net braid. I decided to make the light seams smaller and more frequent so that they would intersect more like rope.

Prototyping Interaction

The effect was still too abstract and didn’t capture the depth of the nets. With help from my roommate, I changed the approach to focus on lighting the negative space between the ropes, and foreshortening the pattern so it created more depth when lit up.

From there, I went more to the interaction design/prototyping and testing the light in the rim. To capture the traditional "sieving oysters" and rolling pearls around a basket, I made the light proportional to an accelerometer to capture that same feeling of "rolling the light around the rim."

Integrated Product System

The last part of prototyping was combining the two to create an IoT system powered by spatial computing. The diving net's light became responsive to the color of the caught pearl, and pearls would be placed centered through the negative space of the rim.
